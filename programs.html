Programs & Services

Support at every step toward independence.

We collaborate with public agencies, employers, schools, libraries, healthcare providers, and community organizations to improve accessibility and opportunity across the region.

01 — Advocacy

Advocacy & Community Involvement

Blind But Not Broken advocates for practical solutions, public awareness, and the removal of barriers impacting blind and visually impaired individuals. Our advocacy efforts include:

  • Reducing barriers to employment
  • Promoting accessible hiring and workplace practices
  • Improving accessibility in public transportation and public facilities
  • Increasing participation in community planning discussions
  • Supporting legislation and policies that improve accessibility and independence
  • Educating businesses and institutions on accessibility practices
  • Removing barriers that prevent formerly incarcerated individuals from obtaining guide dogs and related mobility support services

02 — Orientation & Mobility

Orientation & Mobility (O&M) Training

Training designed to strengthen independence, confidence, and safe navigation throughout the community. Services may include:

  • White cane training
  • Public transportation navigation
  • Pedestrian and street-crossing safety
  • Route planning and travel skills
  • Independent living support
  • Community-based mobility practice

03 — Professional Development

Professional Development

Workforce readiness and economic mobility through professional development services tailored to blind and visually impaired individuals. Programs may include:

  • Resume and interview preparation
  • Workplace readiness training
  • Leadership development
  • Entrepreneurship support
  • Mentorship opportunities
  • Networking and career exploration
  • Digital literacy and remote-work readiness

04 — Technology & Braille

Technology & Braille Training

Braille instruction and accessible technology training to strengthen communication, independence, education, and workforce success. Services may include:

  • Braille literacy instruction
  • Screen reader training
  • Smartphone accessibility training
  • Assistive technology support
  • Adaptive software instruction
  • Digital accessibility education
