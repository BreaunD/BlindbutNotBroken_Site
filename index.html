Our Mission

Advocacy, training, and community — built for independence.

Blind But Not Broken empowers blind and visually impaired individuals through advocacy, mobility training, workforce development, accessible technology, and community-centered support services that promote independence and opportunity.

The Problem

The barriers are real — and they are measurable.

Blind and visually impaired individuals continue to face significant barriers to employment, transportation, education, and community participation. According to the American Foundation for the Blind (AFB):

44%

of working-age individuals who are blind or visually impaired are employed, compared to 79% of individuals without disabilities.

90%

reported accessibility barriers when applying for jobs online.

60%

experienced challenges during workplace onboarding processes.

50M+

American adults report experiencing some level of vision loss or serious difficulty seeing.

Despite these realities, many communities — including Dayton and the surrounding region — lack a coordinated local organization dedicated specifically to blindness and visual impairment services. Blind But Not Broken was created to help fill that gap.

Programs & Services

Four ways we build independence.

Advocacy & Community Involvement

Advocating for practical solutions, public awareness, and the removal of barriers impacting blind and visually impaired individuals.

Orientation & Mobility Training

White cane training, public transportation navigation, street-crossing safety, route planning, and independent living support.

Professional Development

Resume and interview preparation, workplace readiness, leadership development, entrepreneurship support, and remote-work readiness.

Technology & Braille Training

Braille literacy, screen reader training, smartphone accessibility, assistive technology support, and digital accessibility education.
