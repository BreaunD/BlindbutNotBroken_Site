Legal & Disclosures

Transparency you can trust.

We believe donors deserve clear, honest information about who we are and how contributions are handled.

Fiscal sponsorship

Blind But Not Broken operates as a fiscally sponsored project of Felons With A Future, a recognized 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization (EIN [CONFIRM EIN]). Under this arrangement, Felons With A Future receives and administers charitable contributions made in support of Blind But Not Broken's mission, in accordance with their fiscal sponsorship agreement.

Tax-deductibility

Because Blind But Not Broken is supported through a 501(c)(3) fiscal sponsor, donations are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law. No goods or services are provided in exchange for a charitable contribution unless otherwise stated. A written donation receipt is available on request — please contact us using the details below.

Where donations go

All charitable contributions are directed to support the programs and services described on this website: advocacy, orientation and mobility training, professional development, and technology and Braille training for blind and visually impaired individuals in the Dayton region.

Accessibility statement

This website is designed to meet or exceed the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. It is built with semantic structure, keyboard navigation, visible focus indicators, and high-contrast color, and is tested for compatibility with screen readers. If you encounter any barrier using this site, please contact us so we can fix it and provide the information you need in an accessible format.
